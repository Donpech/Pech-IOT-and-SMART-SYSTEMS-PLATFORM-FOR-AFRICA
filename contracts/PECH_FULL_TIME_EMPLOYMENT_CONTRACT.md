## 11. CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE

11.1. **Definition:** "Confidential Information" means all information (whether written, oral, electronic, or in any other form) relating to the business, operations, finances, technology, customers, suppliers, employees, strategies, and affairs of the Employer and the Group, including but not limited to:

   (a) Source code, algorithms, software architecture, APIs, and technical documentation;

   (b) Business plans, financial data, pricing strategies, and marketing plans;

   (c) Customer and supplier lists, contact details, and transactional data;

   (d) Trade secrets, know-how, and proprietary processes;

   (e) Employee data, salary information, and HR records;

   (f) Product roadmaps, research and development plans;

   (g) Any information marked or identified as confidential;

   (h) Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential.

11.2. **Obligations:** The Employee shall:

   (a) Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential and not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the Employer;

   (b) Use Confidential Information only for the proper performance of their duties;

   (c) Take all reasonable measures to protect the confidentiality of such information;

   (d) Not copy, reproduce, or store Confidential Information except as necessary for their duties.

11.3. **Duration:** The Employee's obligations under this Clause:

   (a) Shall apply during the term of employment and indefinitely thereafter for trade secrets;

   (b) Shall apply for a period of five (5) years after termination for all other Confidential Information.

11.4. **Exceptions:** The obligations in this Clause shall not apply to information that:

   (a) Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Employee;

   (b) Was already known to the Employee before employment, as evidenced by written records;

   (c) Is independently developed by the Employee after termination without reference to Confidential Information;

   (d) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided the Employee gives the Employer prompt written notice and cooperates with any efforts to obtain a protective order.

11.5. **Return of Materials:** Upon termination of employment or at any time upon the Employer's request, the Employee shall immediately return all documents, electronic files, equipment, and materials containing or relating to Confidential Information, and shall permanently delete all electronic copies in their possession or control.

## 12. NON-COMPETITION

12.1. The Employee agrees that during the term of employment and for a period of **twelve (12) months** after the termination of employment (the "Restriction Period"), the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, without the prior written consent of the Employer:

   (a) Be employed by, engaged by, or provide services to any business that is in direct competition with the business of the Employer or the Group within the Restricted Territory;

   (b) Establish, own, manage, operate, or participate in any business that competes with the Employer or the Group within the Restricted Territory;

   (c) Assist any person or entity in competing with the Employer or the Group within the Restricted Territory.

12.2. **Restricted Territory:** Nigeria.

12.3. **Competing Business:** A business that is a direct competitor to the Employer in the IoT and smart systems sector, meaning a business whose primary activity is the development, deployment, or sale of IoT platforms, smart home/building automation systems, or smart infrastructure monitoring solutions that directly compete with PECH's products and services. For the avoidance of doubt, general technology employment (including employment in software development, cloud computing, data analytics, e-commerce, fintech, or other technology sectors that do not directly compete with the Employer's core IoT and smart systems business) shall not constitute employment with a Competing Business.

12.4. **Garden Leave:** During the Restriction Period, the Employer may place the Employee on garden leave, during which the Employee shall remain employed and shall receive full salary and benefits but shall not be required to attend work or perform duties.

12.5. **Reasonableness:** The Parties agree that the restrictions in this Clause are reasonable and necessary to protect the legitimate business interests of the Employer. If any court of competent jurisdiction determines that any restriction is unreasonable, the court may reduce the scope, duration, or territory of the restriction to the extent necessary to make it enforceable.

12.6. **Consideration:** The Employee acknowledges that the employment and remuneration under this Contract constitute adequate consideration for the restrictions in this Clause. In the event that additional consideration is required by law, the Employer shall pay the Employee fifty percent (50%) of their monthly basic salary for each month of the Restriction Period.

## 13. NON-SOLICITATION

13.1. The Employee agrees that they shall not, at any time after the termination of employment, directly or indirectly:

   (a) Solicit, entice away, or attempt to solicit or entice away from the Employer or the Group any employee, contractor, consultant, or agent;

   (b) Solicit, canvass, or approach any customer, client, or supplier of the Employer or the Group with whom the Employee had dealings during the last twelve (12) months of employment, for the purpose of offering competing products or services;

   (c) Interfere with the relationship between the Employer and any of its employees, contractors, customers, or suppliers.

13.2. **Duration:** The restrictions in this Clause shall apply in perpetuity. The Parties acknowledge that ongoing protection of the Employer's business relationships is essential to the Employer's legitimate interests.

13.3. **Severability:** If a court of competent jurisdiction determines that the perpetual duration in Clause 13.2 is unreasonable, the court is requested to substitute the maximum duration that it considers reasonable and enforceable.

14.1. The Employee shall not, during the term of employment or at any time thereafter, without the prior written approval of the Chief Executive Officer or designated Communications Officer of the Employer:

   (a) Give any public interview, press statement, or media appearance relating to the business, operations, or affairs of the Employer or the Group;

   (b) Post, publish, or share on any social media platform, blog, website, podcast, or other public forum any information relating to the Employer or the Group;

   (c) Participate in any conference, seminar, panel discussion, or speaking engagement where information about the Employer or the Group may be discussed;

   (d) Author or co-author any article, paper, or publication that references the Employer or the Group.

14.3. **Permitted Disclosures:** The Employee may disclose their job title and a general description of their role (e.g., for LinkedIn profiles or professional networking), but shall not disclose any Confidential Information or specific details about the Employer's projects, clients, technology, or strategy.

14.4. **Duration:** The restrictions in this Clause shall apply during the term of employment and for a period of **twenty-four (24) months** after termination. For the avoidance of doubt, the obligation to protect Confidential Information under Clause 11 (which has its own survival periods) remains separate from and unaffected by this media restriction period.

14.5. **Breach:** Any unauthorized public communication shall constitute Gross Misconduct and may result in summary dismissal and/or legal action for damages.

14.6. The Employee acknowledges that they have read and understood the **PECH Media and Public Communications Policy** and agrees to be bound by its terms.
