## 1. Purpose & Objectives

### 1.1 Purpose

This Employee Stock Ownership Plan ("ESOP" or "the Plan") establishes the framework for granting equity participation to eligible employees and key contributors of PECH Group Holdings Ltd ("the Company"). The Plan is designed to:

- **Attract** top-tier technology and business talent in a competitive Nigerian and African market
- **Retain** key employees through meaningful long-term equity incentives
- **Align** employee interests with shareholder value creation
- **Reward** sustained contribution and loyalty over a 5-year commitment period
- **Build** a culture of ownership and accountability across all verticals

### 1.2 Objectives

| Objective | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Talent Attraction | Competitive equity packages for senior and critical roles |
| Retention | 2-year cliff ensures minimum commitment before any vesting |
| Long-term Alignment | 5-year total vesting ties rewards to sustained performance |
| Shareholder Protection | Company buyback right prevents unwanted external shareholders |
| Pool Sustainability | Unvested shares return to pool, ensuring ongoing availability |

| Category | Eligibility | Typical Allocation Range |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|
| **C-Level Executives** (CTO, CFO, COO) | Automatic consideration upon hiring | 1.0% — 3.0% |

### 4.2 Ineligible Persons

The following are NOT eligible for ESOP grants:

- Interns (eligible for stipend-based compensation only)
- Contract workers (unless converted to full-time employment)
- Advisory Board members (compensated separately)
- External consultants
- Shareholders who already hold >5% equity

### 4.3 Eligibility Criteria

To receive a grant, a Participant must:

1. Be a confirmed full-time employee of the Company (probation completed)
2. Have signed the PECH Full-Time Employment Contract
3. Have signed the IP Assignment and Confidentiality Agreement
4. Not be subject to any active disciplinary proceedings
5. Be recommended by their Vertical Lead and approved by the ESOP Committee

## 6. Vesting Schedule — 2-Year Cliff, 5-Year Vesting

### 6.1 Vesting Terms

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Total Vesting Period** | 5 years (60 months) from Grant Date |
| **Cliff Period** | 2 years (24 months) — NO vesting occurs before this date |
| **Cliff Vesting** | 40% of total grant vests on the Cliff Date (Month 24) |
| **Post-Cliff Vesting** | Remaining 60% vests monthly in equal installments over 36 months |
| **Monthly Post-Cliff Vest** | 1.667% of total grant per month (60% ÷ 36 months) |
| **Full Vesting Date** | 5 years (60 months) from Grant Date |

### 6.2 Vesting Schedule Table

| Period | Month | Cumulative Vested % | Newly Vested | Status |
|--------|-------|--------------------:|-------------:|--------|
| **Cliff Period** | 0 — 23 | **0%** | 0% | Nothing vests |
| **Cliff Date** | 24 | **40%** | 40% | Cliff vests |
| **Post-Cliff Year 3** | 25 — 36 | 40% — 60% | ~1.67%/mo | Monthly vesting |
| **Post-Cliff Year 4** | 37 — 48 | 60% — 80% | ~1.67%/mo | Monthly vesting |
| **Post-Cliff Year 5** | 49 — 60 | 80% — **100%** | ~1.67%/mo | Monthly vesting |

### 6.3 Example: 100,000 Share Grant

| Milestone | Month | Shares Vested (Period) | Cumulative Vested | Unvested |
|-----------|-------|----------------------:|-----------------:|--------:|
| Grant Date | 0 | 0 | 0 | 100,000 |
| End of Year 1 | 12 | 0 | 0 | 100,000 |
| **Cliff Date** | **24** | **40,000** | **40,000** | **60,000** |
| End of Year 3 | 36 | 20,000 | 60,000 | 40,000 |
| End of Year 4 | 48 | 20,000 | 80,000 | 20,000 |
| **Full Vest** | **60** | **20,000** | **100,000** | **0** |

### 6.4 Key Rules

1. **No partial cliff vesting** — if a Participant leaves at Month 23 (one month before cliff), they receive ZERO shares
2. **Monthly post-cliff vesting** — after the cliff, shares vest on the last business day of each calendar month
3. **Continuous employment required** — vesting requires uninterrupted full-time employment
4. **Approved leave** — board-approved leave (maternity, medical, etc.) does not pause the vesting clock
5. **Unpaid leave >30 days** — vesting clock pauses for the duration of unpaid leave exceeding 30 consecutive days

### 9.2 Good Leaver Events

A Participant shall be classified as a **Good Leaver** if their departure results from:

1. **Voluntary resignation** after completing the 2-year cliff period
2. **Redundancy** — position eliminated due to business restructuring
3. **Mutual agreement** — separation agreed by both Company and Participant
4. **Death** of the Participant (Options pass to estate)
5. **Permanent disability** — as certified by a qualified medical practitioner
6. **Retirement** — at or after the statutory retirement age
7. **Constructive dismissal** — as determined by a court or arbitration

**Good Leaver Treatment:**
- **Vested Options:** Retained — Participant has **12 months** from departure to exercise
- **Unvested Options:** Forfeited — **ALL unvested shares immediately return to the ESOP Pool**
- **Post-exercise:** Shares held subject to Company ROFR (Section 10)

### 9.3 Bad Leaver Events

A Participant shall be classified as a **Bad Leaver** if their departure results from:

1. **Dismissal for Gross Misconduct** (as defined in the Employment Contract, Clause 17.3)
2. **Resignation before the Cliff Date** (Month 24)
3. **Material breach** of the Employment Contract, NDA, or IP Assignment
4. **Fraud, theft, or dishonesty** related to Company business
5. **Breach of non-competition** or non-solicitation obligations
6. **Unauthorized disclosure** of Confidential Information
7. **Conviction of a criminal offense** related to Company business

**Bad Leaver Treatment:**
- **ALL Options (vested AND unvested):** **FORFEITED** — 100% of shares return to the ESOP Pool
- **No exercise period** — all rights terminate immediately on the date of departure
- **No compensation** — the Participant receives no payment for forfeited Options

### 9.4 Unvested Shares — Mandatory Pool Return

**This is a fundamental rule of the Plan:**

> Any Participant who departs the Company before completing the full 5-year vesting period shall forfeit ALL unvested Options. These unvested shares shall **immediately and automatically return to the ESOP Pool** and become available for new grants to future eligible employees.

**No exceptions.** The Board has no discretion to accelerate vesting for departing employees except in the specific circumstances set out in Section 15 (Change of Control).

## 10. Company Right of First Refusal (Buyback)

### 10.1 ROFR Principle

**The Company shall have the Right of First Refusal (ROFR) to purchase any vested Shares before a Participant may sell to any third party.** This ensures the Company maintains control over its shareholder register and prevents unwanted external shareholders.

### 10.2 ROFR Process

```
+=========================================================================+
|                    COMPANY BUYBACK (ROFR) PROCESS                       |
+=========================================================================+

  STEP 1: PARTICIPANT INTENT TO SELL
  +-------------------------------------------------------------------+
  |  Participant notifies Company in writing of intent to sell        |
  |  vested Shares, including:                                        |
  |  - Number of Shares to be sold                                    |
  |  - Proposed price per Share (or third-party offer, if any)        |
  |  - Identity of proposed buyer (if applicable)                     |
  +-------------------------------------------------------------------+
                                    |
                                    v
  STEP 2: COMPANY EVALUATION (30 BUSINESS DAYS)
  +-------------------------------------------------------------------+
  |  Company/Board evaluates the sale notice:                          |
  |  - Determines Fair Market Value (FMV) if not already established  |
  |  - Assesses whether to exercise ROFR                               |
  |  - May commission independent valuation if needed                  |
  +-------------------------------------------------------------------+
                                    |
                    +---------------+---------------+
                    |                               |
              Company EXERCISES              Company DECLINES
              ROFR (buys shares)             ROFR
                    |                               |
                    v                               v
  +---------------------------+     +---------------------------+
  |  STEP 3A: BUYBACK         |     |  STEP 3B: EXTERNAL SALE   |
  |                           |     |                           |
  |  Company purchases at     |     |  Participant may sell to  |
  |  FMV or agreed price      |     |  approved third party at  |
  |                           |     |  price >= ROFR offer      |
  |  Shares return to ESOP    |     |                           |
  |  Pool or Treasury         |     |  Subject to Board         |
  |                           |     |  approval of buyer        |
  |  Payment within 60 days   |     |                           |
  +---------------------------+     +---------------------------+
```

### 10.3 Buyback Terms

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **ROFR Notice Period** | Participant must give 30 business days written notice |
| **Company Response Time** | 30 business days from receipt of notice |
| **Buyback Price** | Fair Market Value as determined by: (a) Board valuation, (b) independent valuation, or (c) most recent funding round price |
| **Payment Terms** | Within 60 calendar days of exercise, by bank transfer |
| **Partial Exercise** | Company may exercise ROFR for all or part of the offered Shares |
| **Third-Party Sale** | Only permitted if Company declines ROFR AND Board approves the buyer |
| **Sale Price Floor** | Third-party sale must be at price equal to or greater than ROFR offer |
| **Restriction Period** | If Company declines, Participant has 90 days to complete third-party sale |

### 10.4 Buyback Pricing Methodology

```
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
|              FAIR MARKET VALUE (FMV) DETERMINATION                |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                                  |
|  Method 1: BOARD VALUATION (Pre-Revenue / Early Stage)           |
|  - Board determines FMV based on company metrics                 |
|  - Updated annually or at material events                        |
|  - Must be documented and defensible                             |
|                                                                  |
|  Method 2: INDEPENDENT VALUATION (Growth Stage)                  |
|  - Commissioned from qualified firm (Big 4 or equivalent)        |
|  - Uses Discounted Cash Flow, Comparable Companies, or           |
|    Precedent Transactions methodologies                          |
|  - Cost borne by the Company                                     |
|                                                                  |
|  Method 3: MOST RECENT FUNDING ROUND (Post-Investment)           |
|  - Price per share from the most recent arm's-length             |
|    equity investment                                             |
|  - Subject to any liquidity or minority discount                 |
|  - Valid for 12 months from the funding round close              |
|                                                                  |
+------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### 10.5 Company's Right to Buy Back on Departure

In addition to the general ROFR, the Company has the **specific right to repurchase vested Shares from departing Good Leavers**:

- **Offer Period:** Company must make a buyback offer within 60 days of departure
- **Acceptance Period:** Departing employee has 30 days to accept or decline
- **If Declined:** Employee retains Shares but remains subject to general ROFR
- **Price:** Fair Market Value at time of departure

### 11.3 Pool Sustainability

The recycling mechanism ensures the ESOP Pool remains sustainable:

- **Early departures** (before cliff) return 100% of shares to the pool
- **Mid-term departures** return proportional unvested shares
- **Company buybacks** return shares for re-granting
- **Expired options** return to the available pool

## 12. Exercise of Options

### 12.1 Exercise Rights

| Situation | Exercise Right |
|-----------|---------------|
| **During Employment** | Vested Options may be exercised at any time after the Cliff Date |
| **Good Leaver** | 12 months from departure date to exercise vested Options |
| **Bad Leaver** | NO exercise rights — all Options forfeited immediately |
| **Death** | Estate has 12 months to exercise vested Options |
| **Disability** | 12 months from disability determination to exercise |

### 12.2 Exercise Process

1. Participant submits **Exercise Notice** to the ESOP Committee
2. Participant pays the **Exercise Price** (number of shares × exercise price per share)
3. Company verifies vesting status and Exercise Price payment
4. Company issues **Share Certificate** within 30 business days
5. Shares are registered in the Company's **Share Register**
6. Participant becomes a **Class A Shareholder** with voting and dividend rights

### 12.3 Cashless Exercise (If Available)

The Board may, at its discretion, permit a **cashless exercise** where:
- The Participant exercises Options without upfront cash payment
- The Exercise Price is deducted from the value of the Shares issued
- Fewer Shares are issued to cover the Exercise Price

## 13. Transfer Restrictions

### 13.1 Lock-Up Period

- **Post-exercise lock-up:** 6 months from date of exercise — no transfers permitted
- **During employment:** Shares may not be sold, transferred, pledged, or encumbered without Board approval

### 13.2 Permitted Transfers

Shares may only be transferred to:

1. **The Company** (via ROFR or buyback)
2. **Existing shareholders** (with Board approval and subject to pre-emption rights)
3. **Spouse or immediate family trust** (with Board approval, shares remain subject to ROFR)
4. **Board-approved third parties** (only after Company declines ROFR)

### 13.3 Prohibited Transfers

Shares may NOT be:

- Sold on any public market (unless Company is publicly listed)
- Used as collateral for loans without Board approval
- Transferred to competitors or persons on any Company restricted list
- Subject to any lien, charge, or encumbrance without Board consent

## 15. Change of Control & Liquidity Events

### 15.1 Accelerated Vesting on Change of Control

In the event of a **Change of Control** (as defined below), the Board may, at its discretion, accelerate vesting of all or part of unvested Options:

| Change of Control Event | Acceleration | Condition |
|------------------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Acquisition** (>50% shares acquired by third party) | Board discretion: 0-100% acceleration | Subject to acquirer's agreement |
| **Merger** | Board discretion: 0-100% acceleration | Subject to merger terms |
| **IPO** | No automatic acceleration | Options become tradeable post-lock-up |
| **Asset Sale** (>75% of company assets) | Board discretion: 0-100% acceleration | Pro-rata to other shareholders |

### 15.2 Double-Trigger Acceleration

For **senior roles** (C-Level and VP), the Plan provides **double-trigger acceleration**:

1. **First Trigger:** Change of Control event occurs
2. **Second Trigger:** Participant is terminated without cause OR resigns for good reason within 12 months of the Change of Control

If both triggers occur, **100% of unvested Options accelerate immediately**.

### 15.3 IPO Provisions

- Options convert to publicly tradeable shares (subject to exchange rules)
- **180-day post-IPO lock-up** for all ESOP Participants
- Class A shares maintain equal economic rights
- Board may impose additional trading restrictions during blackout periods
