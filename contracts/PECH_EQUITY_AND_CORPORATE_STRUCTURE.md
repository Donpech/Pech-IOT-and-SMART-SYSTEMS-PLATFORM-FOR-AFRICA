## 3. Dual-Class Share Structure

### 3.1 Legal Basis

CAMA 2020, Section 142 permits Nigerian companies to issue shares with **different voting rights**. The Articles of Association must specify the rights attached to each class.

### 3.2 Class B Super-Voting Shares

| Feature | Class A (Ordinary) | Class B (Super-Voting) |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Votes per share** | 1 | 10 |
| **Holder(s)** | Ndu, ESOP, Strategic Reserve | Founder only |
| **Dividend rights** | Equal per share | Equal per share |
| **Transferability** | Subject to pre-emption | Non-transferable (auto-converts to Class A on transfer) |
| **Conversion** | N/A | Class B → Class A at holder's option (irreversible) |

### 3.3 Sunset Provisions

Class B shares shall **automatically convert** to Class A shares upon:

1. Death or permanent incapacitation of the Founder (unless transferred to a Founder-controlled trust)
2. Voluntary conversion by the Founder
3. IPO (if required by listing exchange rules)
4. 15 years from incorporation (sunset clause)

### 3.4 Rationale

The dual-class structure protects the Founder's long-term vision for PECH while ensuring equal economic participation for all shareholders. This is standard practice for technology companies (used by Google, Meta, Snap, Grab, etc.).

## 6. Vertical-Level Governance

### 6.1 Principle

**Only people involved in a vertical vote on that vertical's operational matters.** Company-level decisions follow the share structure above, but vertical-specific decisions are governed by vertical committees.

### 6.2 Vertical Decision Matrix

| Decision Type | Who Votes | Threshold |
|---------------|----------|-----------|
| **Company-level** (equity changes, M&A, budget >₦10M, new verticals) | All shareholders per share class | Per Articles of Association |
| **Vertical strategy** (product roadmap, pricing, partnerships within vertical) | Vertical Lead + Vertical Committee + CEO | Majority of vertical committee |
| **Vertical hiring** (roles within the vertical) | Vertical Lead + CEO approval | Consensus |
| **Vertical budget** (within pre-approved annual allocation) | Vertical Lead + CFO/CEO | Vertical Lead proposes, CEO approves |

## 10. Funding Obligations & Cessation

### 10.1 Ndu's Funding Obligations

Ndu Jerry Chijioke's 40% equity allocation reflects his commitment to fund the business operations. The specific terms:

- **Obligation:** Provide capital contributions to fund PECH operations as mutually agreed
- **Timing:** Capital calls as needed per the business plan and budget
- **Documentation:** Each capital contribution shall be documented with a board resolution

### 10.2 Cessation of Funding

If Ndu is unable or unwilling to continue funding:

1. **No penalty** — Ndu retains all shares already issued
2. **No forced dilution** — his existing equity is not clawed back
3. **Strategic Reserve activation** — the 10% Strategic Reserve pool becomes available to raise external capital from new investors or strategic partners in specific verticals requiring equity participation
4. **Pre-emption right** — Ndu has right of first refusal on any new share issuance from the Strategic Reserve
5. **Board notification** — Ndu must provide 60 days written notice of intent to cease funding

### 10.3 External Capital Raising

When external capital is needed (either due to Ndu's funding cessation or growth requirements):

- New shares issued from the 10% Strategic Reserve pool
- All existing shareholders have pro-rata pre-emption rights
- Class A shares only (no new Class B shares)
- Valuation determined by independent valuation or negotiation

## 14. Transfer Restrictions & Pre-Emption

### 14.1 Lock-Up Period

- **Both Founders:** 24-month lock-up from incorporation (no transfers)
- **ESOP holders:** Subject to vesting schedule + 6-month post-vesting hold

### 14.2 Pre-Emption Rights

Any shareholder wishing to sell must:

1. Offer shares to existing shareholders first (pro-rata)
2. Allow 30 business days for acceptance
3. If not fully subscribed, offer to the Company
4. Only then may sell to third parties (at same or higher price)

### 14.3 Class B Transfer Restriction

**Class B shares automatically convert to Class A upon any transfer** (except to a Founder-controlled trust or estate planning vehicle). This ensures super-voting power cannot be sold or transferred to third parties.

## 15. Deadlock & Dispute Resolution

### 15.1 Escalation Process

1. **Good faith negotiation** between the parties (14 days)
2. **Mediation** through Lagos Multi-Door Courthouse (30 days)
3. **Arbitration** under the Arbitration and Mediation Act 2023, seated in Lagos
4. **Court proceedings** only if arbitration fails (Federal High Court, Lagos Division)

### 15.2 Deadlock Resolution

In the event of a board deadlock:
- The Founder's vote shall be the casting vote (due to Class B voting control)
- For matters requiring Ndu's protective consent (Section 11.1), deadlock triggers mediation

## 16. Protective Provisions (Veto Rights)

### 16.1 Matters Requiring Unanimous Shareholder Consent

The following matters require consent of **both** the Founder and Ndu (regardless of voting power):

1. Amendment to the Articles of Association affecting share class rights
2. Issuance of any new Class B shares
3. Voluntary dissolution of the Company
4. Sale of substantially all Company assets
5. Merger or acquisition where shareholders receive less than 2× their investment
6. Change in the Company's primary line of business
7. Related-party transactions exceeding ₦5,000,000

### 16.2 Matters Requiring Board Supermajority (≥4 of 5 Directors)

1. Annual budget approval
2. Capital expenditure exceeding ₦25,000,000
3. Entry into new geographic markets
4. Hiring of C-level executives
5. Material changes to compensation structure

## 17. Exit & Liquidity Events

### 17.1 Drag-Along Rights

If shareholders holding ≥75% of votes approve a sale, all other shareholders must participate on the same terms.

### 17.2 Tag-Along Rights

If the Founder sells ≥20% of his shares, Ndu has the right to sell a proportional amount on the same terms.

### 17.3 IPO Provisions

- Class B shares may convert to Class A (per exchange requirements)
- 180-day post-IPO lock-up for all founding shareholders
- Registration rights for Ndu's shares

### 17.4 Buy-Sell (Shotgun) Clause

Either founding shareholder may trigger a buy-sell:
1. Offering party names a price per share
2. Receiving party must either buy at that price or sell at that price
3. 60 days to complete the transaction
