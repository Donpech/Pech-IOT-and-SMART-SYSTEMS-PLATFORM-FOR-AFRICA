## 5. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

### 5.1 Relationship of the Parties

(a) The Contractor is an **independent contractor** and **not** an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as creating an employer-employee relationship, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship between the Parties.

(b) This Agreement is a contract for services and shall not be governed by the provisions of the Labour Act (Cap L1, Laws of the Federation of Nigeria, 2004) or any other employment legislation that applies exclusively to employer-employee relationships.

### 5.2 No Employment Rights

The Contractor expressly acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) The Contractor has **no entitlement** to any rights, privileges, or benefits available to employees of the Company under Nigerian law, including but not limited to:

   - Protection against unfair dismissal;
   - Redundancy payments;
   - Minimum wage entitlements;
   - Statutory leave entitlements;
   - Pension contributions by the Company;
   - NHIA contributions by the Company;
   - Group life insurance coverage;
   - Any other statutory employment benefits;

(b) The Contractor shall not hold themselves out as an employee of the Company and shall not make any representations to third parties that would suggest an employment relationship;

(c) The Contractor shall not have authority to bind the Company, enter into contracts on behalf of the Company, or create any obligation or liability on behalf of the Company without the Company's express prior written consent;

(d) The Contractor is free to determine the manner and method of performing the Services, subject to meeting the agreed specifications, deadlines, and quality standards.

### 5.3 Tax Obligations

(a) The Contractor is solely responsible for:

   - Registering with the relevant tax authority (FIRS or State Internal Revenue Service, as applicable);
   - Obtaining and maintaining a valid Tax Identification Number (TIN);
   - Filing all required tax returns;
   - Paying all taxes, levies, and contributions due on the Fees and any other income arising from this Agreement;
   - Compliance with all applicable tax laws and regulations in Nigeria.

(b) The Contractor shall indemnify and hold harmless the Company from and against any claims, penalties, interest, or liabilities arising from the Contractor's failure to comply with tax obligations.

### 5.4 No Workers' Compensation

The Contractor acknowledges that the Company shall not provide workers' compensation insurance or any other form of insurance coverage for the Contractor. The Contractor is solely responsible for obtaining any insurance coverage the Contractor deems necessary.

### 5.5 Tools and Equipment

(a) Unless otherwise specified in Schedule A, the Contractor shall provide all tools, equipment, software, materials, and resources necessary for the performance of the Services at the Contractor's own expense.

(b) Where the Company provides any tools, equipment, or access to systems for the Contractor's use during the Engagement:

   - Such provision does not create an employment relationship;
   - The Contractor shall use such tools and equipment solely for the purpose of performing the Services;
   - The Contractor shall return all Company-provided tools, equipment, and materials promptly upon termination or expiration of this Agreement;
   - The Contractor shall be liable for any loss of or damage to Company-provided tools and equipment caused by the Contractor's negligence or misuse.

(b) The Contractor shall not incorporate any third-party Intellectual Property (including open-source software) into the Work Product without the Company's prior written approval and full disclosure of any applicable licence terms.

### 6.5 Further Assurances

The Contractor agrees to execute all documents, instruments, and agreements, and to take all further actions, as may be reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, register, or enforce the Company's rights in the Work Product, including but not limited to executing assignments, declarations, and applications for registration of Intellectual Property rights. ## 7. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 7.1 Definition of Confidential Information

**"Confidential Information"** means all information, whether written, oral, electronic, or in any other form, that is disclosed by or on behalf of the Company to the Contractor, or that the Contractor obtains or has access to in connection with the Services, including but not limited to:

(a) Trade Secrets, proprietary technology, algorithms, source code, technical specifications, system architectures, and product roadmaps;

(b) Business plans, strategies, financial information, pricing, customer lists, supplier information, marketing plans, and sales data;

(c) Information relating to the Company's IoT platforms, smart systems, solar energy solutions, and related technologies;

(d) Employee and contractor information, organisational charts, compensation data, and human resources policies;

(e) Information about the Company's partners, investors, clients, and business relationships;

(f) Any information designated as "confidential," "proprietary," or with similar markings;

(g) Any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information does not include information that: (i) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Contractor; (ii) was known to the Contractor prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records; (iii) is independently developed by the Contractor without reference to the Confidential Information; or (iv) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure.

### 7.2 Obligations

The Contractor agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not disclose it to any third party without the Company's prior written consent;

(b) Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing the Services under this Agreement;

(c) Take all reasonable measures, at least equivalent to those the Contractor uses to protect their own confidential information (but in no event less than reasonable care), to prevent the unauthorised disclosure, dissemination, or use of the Confidential Information;

(d) Immediately notify the Company in writing upon becoming aware of any unauthorised access to, disclosure of, or use of the Confidential Information;

(e) Not copy, reproduce, or store the Confidential Information except as strictly necessary for the performance of the Services;

(f) Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon request by the Company, promptly return or destroy (at the Company's election) all Confidential Information in the Contractor's possession, including all copies, extracts, and summaries, and certify in writing that such return or destruction has been completed.

### 7.3 Duration of Obligations

(a) The confidentiality obligations with respect to **Trade Secrets** shall continue **in perpetuity**, for so long as the information retains its character as a trade secret.

(b) The confidentiality obligations with respect to all **other Confidential Information** shall continue for a period of **five (5) years** from the date of disclosure or the termination of this Agreement, whichever is later.

### 7.4 Permitted Disclosures

The Contractor may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by:

(a) A valid order of a court or governmental authority of competent jurisdiction; or

(b) Applicable law or regulation;

provided that the Contractor: (i) provides the Company with prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted); (ii) cooperates with the Company in seeking a protective order or other appropriate remedy; and (iii) discloses only the minimum amount of Confidential Information required.

## 8. NON-COMPETITION

### 8.1 Restriction

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of **twelve (12) months** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement (the **"Restricted Period"**), the Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly, whether on their own behalf or on behalf of any other person, firm, company, or entity:

(a) Engage in, carry on, or be concerned with any business that directly competes with the business of the Company or any of its Affiliates within the Federal Republic of Nigeria;

(b) Be employed by, act as a consultant to, or provide services to any entity that is a direct competitor of the Company in the fields of IoT platforms, smart systems, solar energy solutions, or related technologies within the Federal Republic of Nigeria;

(c) Establish, invest in (other than holding less than five percent (5%) of the shares of a publicly listed company), or manage any business that directly competes with the Company's business within the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

### 8.2 Reasonableness

The Contractor acknowledges and agrees that:

(a) The restrictions in this Section are reasonable and necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests, including its Confidential Information, Trade Secrets, client relationships, and business goodwill;

(b) The Contractor has had the opportunity to seek independent legal advice regarding these restrictions;

(c) The restrictions do not impose an undue hardship on the Contractor and the Contractor has sufficient skills and experience to earn a livelihood without breaching these restrictions.

### 8.3 Severability of Restrictions

If any court or tribunal of competent jurisdiction determines that any restriction in this Section is unreasonable or unenforceable in whole or in part, the Parties agree that such restriction shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it reasonable and enforceable, rather than being struck down entirely.

## 9. NON-SOLICITATION

### 9.1 Perpetual Non-Solicitation

The Contractor agrees that, during the term of this Agreement and **in perpetuity** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Contractor shall not, directly or indirectly:

(a) Solicit, induce, encourage, or attempt to solicit, induce, or encourage any employee, contractor, officer, director, or consultant of the Company or any of its Affiliates to leave the service of the Company or to breach any agreement with the Company;

(b) Solicit, divert, or attempt to solicit or divert any client, customer, supplier, or business partner of the Company or any of its Affiliates with whom the Contractor had contact or about whom the Contractor obtained Confidential Information during the Engagement, for the purpose of providing products or services that compete with those of the Company;

(c) Interfere with or disrupt, or attempt to interfere with or disrupt, any business relationship between the Company and any of its clients, customers, suppliers, or business partners.

### 9.2 Severability

(a) If any court or tribunal of competent jurisdiction determines that the perpetual duration of the non-solicitation obligations in this Section is unreasonable or unenforceable, the Parties agree that such obligations shall apply for the maximum period permitted by law, which shall in no event be less than **thirty-six (36) months** from the date of termination or expiration of this Agreement.

(b) If any provision of this Section is found to be unreasonable or unenforceable in any respect, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it reasonable and enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

## 10. MEDIA POLICY

### 10.1 Perpetual Media Restrictions

The Contractor agrees that, during the term of this Agreement and **in perpetuity** following the termination or expiration of this Agreement:

(a) The Contractor shall not give any interview, make any public statement, publish any article, post on any social media platform, create any content, or otherwise communicate to the public or to any media outlet any information concerning:

   - The Company's business, operations, strategies, finances, technology, products, or services;

(b) The Contractor shall comply with all conditions, restrictions, and guidelines specified by the Company in its approval;

The Contractor acknowledges that any breach of this Section may cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy, and the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other equitable remedies in addition to any other remedies available at law.

## 16. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 16.1 Internal Resolution

The Parties agree that, in the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, including any question regarding its existence, validity, interpretation, performance, or termination (a **"Dispute"**), the Parties shall first attempt to resolve the Dispute through good-faith negotiation between the Contractor and the Company's designated representative within **fourteen (14) days** of written notice of the Dispute.

### 16.2 Mediation

If the Dispute is not resolved through internal negotiation within the period specified in Section 16.1, either Party may refer the Dispute to mediation. The mediation shall be:

(a) Conducted in Lagos, Nigeria;

(b) Administered by a mutually agreed mediator, or failing agreement, a mediator appointed by the Lagos Court of Arbitration (LCA);

(c) Conducted in the English language;

(d) Completed within thirty (30) days of the appointment of the mediator, unless the Parties agree to extend the period.

### 16.3 Arbitration

If the Dispute is not resolved through mediation within the period specified in Section 16.2, either Party may refer the Dispute to binding arbitration. The arbitration shall be:

(a) Conducted in **Lagos, Nigeria**;

(b) Administered under the rules of the **Arbitration and Mediation Act, 2023** of Nigeria and the rules of the Lagos Court of Arbitration;

(c) Conducted by a sole arbitrator mutually agreed upon by the Parties, or failing agreement within fourteen (14) days, appointed by the President of the Lagos Court of Arbitration;

(d) Conducted in the **English language**;

(e) The decision of the arbitrator shall be **final and binding** on the Parties and may be enforced in any court of competent jurisdiction.

### 16.4 Costs

(a) Each Party shall bear its own costs in connection with the internal negotiation and mediation stages.

(b) The costs of arbitration (including the arbitrator's fees) shall be borne by the Parties as determined by the arbitrator, or in the absence of such determination, equally by the Parties.

(c) The arbitrator may award costs (including reasonable legal fees) to the prevailing Party.

### 16.5 Injunctive Relief

Notwithstanding the foregoing, nothing in this Section shall prevent either Party from seeking urgent injunctive or interim relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to protect its rights under this Agreement, including but not limited to the protection of Confidential Information, Intellectual Property, and enforcement of the restrictive covenants in Sections 8, 9, and 10.
